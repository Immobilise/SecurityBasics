Social Engineering

Social engineering is a tactic used by cybercriminals to manipulate individuals into divulging confidential information or performing actions that may compromise security.

Overview

  • Definition: Social engineering involves exploiting human psychology to gain access to systems, networks, or sensitive information.
  • Common Techniques: Phishing, pretexting, baiting, tailgating, and quid pro quo are some common social engineering techniques.
  • Targets: Individuals, employees, and organizations are often targeted through various communication channels, including email, phone calls, and social media.

Importance of Communication Security

Communication security is crucial in today's digital landscape, where information exchange occurs across various platforms. Ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of communication channels is essential for safeguarding sensitive data and maintaining trust.

Overview

  • Secure Communication: Protecting communication channels from unauthorized access and interception is paramount to prevent data breaches and privacy violations.
  • Encryption: Encrypting communication data ensures that it remains confidential and cannot be deciphered by unauthorized parties.
  • Secure Platforms: Using trusted and secure platforms for messaging, email, video conferencing, and social media minimizes the risk of eavesdropping and tampering.
